<h1>Alaa’s Critical Phase Equation</h1>

<p><strong>Author:</strong> Alaa Sheikh Al-Basatneh<br>
<strong>Title:</strong> Independent Syrian Researcher<br>
<strong>Description:</strong> A proposed spectral-phase model that approximates the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function using a novel phase-critical formulation.</p>

<h2>1. Formal Equation</h2>
<div class="math-block">
  \[
    s_k(A, r, \theta) = \frac{1}{2} + i \cdot \text{Im}(s_k^0(A)) + r \cdot (t_k - \text{Im}(s_k^0(A))) \cdot e^{i\theta}
  \]
  where:
  \[
    s_k^0(A) = \frac{1}{2} + i \cdot \left( \frac{2\pi - \sin(\beta t_k(A)) - \gamma \ln A + G(t_k(A))}{f} \right)
  \]
  and:
  \[
    G(t) = \frac{t}{2} \cdot \ln\left(\frac{t}{2\pi}\right) - \frac{t}{2} - \frac{\pi}{8}
  \]
</div>

<h2>2. Constants</h2>
<ul>
  <li>\(\beta = 1\)</li>
  <li>\(\gamma = 1\)</li>
  <li>\(f = 1\)</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. Explanation</h2>
<p>
This equation generates a complex spectral point \( s_k \) that aligns closely with the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function. The model introduces:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>A phase-driven oscillation component \( \sin(\beta t) \)</li>
  <li>A logarithmic scaling \( \ln A \)</li>
  <li>A correction term \( G(t) \), equivalent to the Riemann–Siegel phase</li>
</ul>
<p>
The parameter \( t_k \) is selected to match the k-th zero approximation index, and the spatial phase transformation \( r, \theta \) allows exploring deformations or resonances in the complex plane.
</p>

<h2>4. Purpose</h2>
<p>
This model proposes a structured, phase-coherent approximation method to explore the spectral nature of the Riemann zeta function's critical line zeros. It supports the hypothesis that all non-trivial zeros lie on the critical line \( \text{Re}(s) = \frac{1}{2} \).
</p>
